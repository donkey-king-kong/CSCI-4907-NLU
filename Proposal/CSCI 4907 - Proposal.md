# CSCI-4907-NLU Project

## 1. Introduction
### Motivation
> Social media platforms have become ubiquitous in our everyday communication as it allows the exchange of ideas, opinions, and information. However, because of its widespread use, it has also resulted in an increase prevalence of online harassment like cyberbullying.
>
> Given this, detecting cyberbullying on social media presents several challenges. For example, harmful content is often expressed through subtle linguistic cues like sarcasm and contextual ambiguity.
>
> Additionally, cyberbullying also frequently occurs in different forms - sometimes at the same time. For example, a single post may contain insults, threats and/or implicit aggression. This therefore introduces a multilabel classification challenge, where models need to learn to assign multiple categories to a single piece of text.
>
> Therefore, because of these characteristics, traditional keyword filtering methods becomes difficult to accurately identify cyberbullying behaviour.
>
> With these complexities, we aim to utilize different machine learning approaches for multilabel cyberbullying detection in tweets.
